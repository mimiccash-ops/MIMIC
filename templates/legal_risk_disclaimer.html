1. General Risk Warning

Cryptocurrency and derivatives trading carries a high level of risk. Before deciding to trade, you should carefully consider your investment objectives, level of experience, and risk appetite. The possibility exists that you could sustain a loss of some or all of your initial investment.

Do not invest money that you cannot afford to lose. Cryptocurrency trading may not be suitable for all investors.

2. Market Risks

  • Volatility Risk: Cryptocurrency markets are highly volatile. Prices can change rapidly within minutes or seconds, leading to significant gains or losses.
  • Liquidity Risk: Some markets may have low liquidity, making it difficult to enter or exit positions at desired prices.
  • Market Manipulation: Cryptocurrency markets may be susceptible to price manipulation, pump-and-dump schemes, and other fraudulent activities.
  • Gap Risk: Prices may "gap" significantly between trading sessions, causing stop-loss orders to execute at prices far from the set level.

3. Copy Trading Risks

  • Past Performance: Historical trading results are not indicative of future performance. A trader's past success does not guarantee future profits.
  • Execution Differences: Your trades may be executed at different prices than the master account due to slippage, timing, and market conditions.
  • Position Sizing: Position sizes relative to account balance may differ, potentially amplifying losses.
  • Strategy Changes: The trading strategy may change at any time without notice.

4. Leverage Risks

Trading with leverage amplifies both potential profits AND losses. You may lose more than your initial margin deposit.

  • High leverage increases both potential returns and potential losses exponentially
  • Margin calls may force liquidation of positions at unfavorable prices
  • Rapid market movements can exceed margin requirements, resulting in negative balances

5. Technical Risks

  • System Failures: Software bugs, server outages, or network issues may prevent trade execution.
  • Connectivity Issues: Internet connection problems may delay or prevent order submission.
  • Exchange Issues: Third-party exchanges may experience downtime, API failures, or security breaches.
  • Latency: Network delays may cause trades to execute at different prices than expected.

6. Regulatory Risks

  • Cryptocurrency regulations vary by jurisdiction and may change at any time
  • New regulations could restrict or prohibit certain trading activities
  • Your jurisdiction may prohibit cryptocurrency trading entirely
  • Tax obligations vary by jurisdiction and may affect profitability

7. Security Risks

  • API key security is your responsibility
  • Exchange hacks or security breaches may result in loss of funds
  • Phishing attacks and social engineering may compromise your accounts
